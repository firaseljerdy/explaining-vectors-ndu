The Movie Night Predictor

You're building a tiny AI brain that learns your movie taste. First by hand, then in code. No libraries, no magic—just multiplication and addition.

The scenario: You and your friends rate movies on two scales—Action (0–10) and Comedy (0–10). Based on those two numbers, your perceptron will learn to predict whether you'd enjoy the movie (+1) or skip it (−1). You're basically teaching a single neuron your vibe.

Movies you liked (+1) have high scores in at least one category. Movies you disliked (−1) have low scores in both. A straight line through the middle could separate them—that's exactly what the perceptron will find.

PART A

Your perceptron has 3 inputs: x₁ (action), x₂ (comedy), and the bias (always 1). Each input gets its own weight. To start, pick 3 random weights between −1 and +1.

Use your phone's calculator or just pick numbers. Type them below — the rest of the exercise will use these values to check your work.

Feedforward by Hand

3

PART C — TEACH THE NEURON

Training by Hand (3 Rounds)

Now the fun part. For each movie, calculate the error and update the weights. This is how the perceptron learns.

error = desired − guess

w_new = w_old + error × input × lr
Remember: lr = 0.1 for this exercise.
Complete Part B first. Training builds on your feedforward results.

function setup() {
  createCanvas(400, 400);
  brain = new Perceptron(3, 0.1);

function draw() {
  background(30);

  let m = movies[trainIndex];
  brain.train([m.x1, m.x2, 1], m.label);
  trainIndex = (trainIndex + 1) % movies.length;

  for (let mv of movies) {
    let px = map(mv.x1, -1, 1, 40, width - 40);
    let py = map(mv.x2, -1, 1, height - 40, 40);
    let guess = brain.feedforward([mv.x1, mv.x2, 1]);
    let correct = (guess === mv.label);
    stroke(correct ? color(16,185,129) : color(239,68,68));
    strokeWeight(correct ? 2 : 3);
    fill(mv.label === 1 ? color(100,200,255) : color(255,150,80));
    circle(px, py, 16);
  }

  fill(200); noStroke(); textSize(12);
  text("Action →", width/2, height - 10);
  text("Comedy ↑", 8, height/2);
}
